## about me
Eindhoven Cycling Tours-founder Maurice Meijer was born and raised in Eindhoven. To
revalidate from a knee-injury, he starting cycling. What started as a small tour of ten
kilometres, lead to cycling tours to Oxford and Cambridge. In 2016 he founded Eindhoven
Cycling Tours: an initiative to show hidden highlights in Eindhoven and its surroundings by
bike.
ECT is all about connection: making connections between various groups of people that want
to discover the area of Eindhoven by bike. ECT connects inhabitants with a broad spectrum
of natural and cultural diversity, expats with locals, companies with students and so on.
Are you curious about what ECT has to offer to you? Just visit
www.eindhovencyclingtours.com and discover which connection you can make!


## why go cycling?

Go cycling. Right now. Harder than yesterday.Because science doesn’t negotiate.

-   Cycling slashes all-cause mortality risk — people who ride regularly live measurably longer (up to 10–41% lower death risk in large reviews).
-   It carves 24% off your cardiovascular death risk and 16% off cancer compared to sitting in a car.
-   Heart attack, stroke, type 2 diabetes? Incidence drops hard when you pedal consistently.
-   Knees actually thank you: lifelong cyclists show healthier cartilage, less arthritis pain, lower osteoarthritis odds than non-riders.
-   Back & neck pain? Low-impact, upright posture on the bike strengthens core stabilizers, improves spinal alignment, reduces chronic load compared to sitting or bad walking form — many with persistent back/hip issues report real relief from regular riding.
-   Mental health frontline: regular cycling measurably cuts anxiety, depression symptoms, and perceived stress (studies show commuters who bike 4+ days/week have significantly lower stress odds). Endorphins flood, serotonin rises, cortisol falls. The mind quiets when the legs spin.
-   Bonus: better coordination, balance, lung capacity, insulin sensitivity, body composition, sleep quality — almost every system gets a systemic upgrade.
